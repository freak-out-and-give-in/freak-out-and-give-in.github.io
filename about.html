About us

Facts

  • Open 7 days a week
  • 369 acres
  • Popular for birdwatching
  • Home to foxes, deer, dragonflies and butterflies
  • Winter - Wildfowl return here to escape the northern cold
  • Spring - Birds singing
  • Summer - Colourful wetland plants and dragonflies
  • Autumn - First migrant birds

History

  • Historically thrived on agriculture and sea trade
  • Had rich soils supporting sheep farming
  • Had a thriving port that encouraged trade until the canal was built in 1611
  • The canal was originally meant to stimulate growth but it backfired
  • The canal suffered from silting and sea trade moved to other towns
  • In constructing the canal, the River Meon was blocked, which created the wetlands that are now the nature reserve
